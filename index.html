About us

Small independent publisher. Broad creative range.

Founded in London on August 5, 2011, Centrinian publishes fiction and non-fiction for adults and children, with an emphasis on quality, accessibility and original storytelling.

Mission

Centrinian brings a drive and passion for storytelling to readers everywhere. The team focuses on creative and innovative stories filled with mystery, magic and history, while welcoming compelling voices with a fresh vision.

What we do

The company aims to bridge the gap between fresh ideas and a new generation of readers, prioritising quality over quantity and matching authors with their ideal audience.

At a glance

  • Established on August 5, 2011
  • Headquartered in London, UK
  • Adult and children’s fiction and non-fiction
  • Print and digital trade book publishing

Divisions & imprints

Centrinian’s three publishing divisions include Non-Fiction, Fiction and Children’s Books. The company also describes its imprints as specialised brands that help readers identify the kinds of stories and products they love.

Submissions

Built for direct, clear manuscript enquiries.

This section mirrors the current submissions guidance and keeps it in a simpler, easier-to-read GitHub Pages layout.

Current status

General submissions are currently closed.

The site states that general submissions open quarterly in March, June, September and December. A literary agent is not required.

Submissions email: submissions@centrinian.com

General information

  • Only a limited number of books are published each year.
  • Unsolicited manuscripts are accepted during open periods.
  • All material must be new and previously unpublished.
  • If work has been denied, wait six months before resubmitting.
  • Centrinian is a royalty-paying publisher with no editing or publishing fees.
  • Books are published in print, ebook and audio formats.

Genres accepted

Action / Adventure Suspense / Thriller / Crime Fantasy / Sci-Fi Paranormal Children’s Fiction Horror Romance Mystery Historical Fiction Literary Fiction Comic / Graphic Novel Biography Memoir Essays Poetry Self-help

What to send

  • Subject line: Query – Book Title / Name of Author / Book Genre
  • A concise query letter with email address, phone number and relevant background
  • Whether the submission is exclusive or non-exclusive
  • A 1–2 page synopsis
  • The first three chapters
  • For poetry: no more than six original poems

Important restrictions

  • Email queries only
  • Paste submissions in the email body
  • Do not send attachments unless requested
  • No postal submissions
  • No screenplays
  • No unfinished manuscripts except by referral

Response expectations

The site notes that a large volume of submissions is received, so replies may take weeks. Writers are asked not to follow up until 12 weeks have elapsed. An automated acknowledgement email should be sent after receipt.
